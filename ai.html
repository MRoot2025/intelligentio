The five hard problems

The problems no one is fully solving — and how we’re solving them.

Every claim on this page maps to a product we’ve shipped or are shipping. No vaporware. No prophetic marketing.

PROBLEM 01

Multi-agent systems fail in production 41–86% of the time.

Hallucinations cascade across hops. False consensus locks in. The MCP transport that wires it all together has 30+ public CVEs. Specification ambiguity, coordination breakdown, verification gaps — this is the hard one nobody owns.

Our answer: AiT Coord — JWT-signed multi-agent orchestrator with per-tenant scoping, idempotent tool calls, and audit trails for every agent invocation.

PROBLEM 02

LLM security is OWASP’s LLM01 for a reason — nothing fully fixes it.

Prompt injection has no general defense. HuggingFace hosts 1.2M models, July 2025 disclosed an attack vector across 1.5M files. Malicious LoRA adapters and supply-chain poisoning are operational, not theoretical.

Our answer: AiT SOC Sentinel — AI-native SOC that watches the agents themselves: prompt-injection detection, model-supply-chain attestation, anomalous tool-call alerting.

PROBLEM 03

RAG retrieves the wrong document ~40% of the time.

And the LLM still answers confidently and well-formatted. Faithfulness, citation coverage, semantic drift — these are the binding metrics. Vector indexes go stale silently and your compliance Q&A bot answers against last year’s policy doc.

Our answer: AiT Knowledgebase Agent + Trust Portal — citation-bound retrieval, freshness monitoring, and per-tenant index ownership.

PROBLEM 04

Enterprise LLM spend hit $8.4B in 2025. None of it is governed at the MSP layer.

Multi-tenant gateways must enforce per-client quotas, model routing, regional egress, audit logs, and PII redaction. Cloudflare AI Gateway has no RBAC. Portkey is enterprise-direct, not MSP-channel.

Our answer: AiT AI Gateway — LiteLLM control plane + Cloudflare edge: per-tenant virtual keys, spend logs, prompt caching, regional egress, MSP-resold to your clients.

PROBLEM 05

EU AI Act Article 50 hits Aug 2 2026. TRAIGA already fined $200K/violation.

Watermarking, provenance ledgers, and consent retention are now mandatory for AI-generated content in regulated verticals. NIST AI RMF substantial compliance is the affirmative defense. Most MSPs aren’t ready.

Our answer: AiT Audit + AiT Avatar Studio + Trust Portal — continuous audit, watermarked outputs, consent archived 7 years per ELVIS/TRAIGA.

PROBLEM 06 — THE META-PROBLEM

Most MSPs sell AI. Almost none run on the AI they sell.

If your MSP cannot show you their own production agents, audit trail, and governance posture, ask why. We run our entire 24-project codebase under the same systems we ship to clients. Continuous audit. Multi-tenant. Single pane.
