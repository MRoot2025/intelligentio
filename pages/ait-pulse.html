What AiT Pulse monitors

Most marketing tools watch one thing. AiT Pulse watches the four things that compound — because in 2026, your AI-search visibility, your reputation surface, your website performance, and your email deliverability are the same conversation.

  • AI Search Visibility (6 engines). Twenty buyer-intent prompts run weekly against ChatGPT, Perplexity, Google AI Overviews, Gemini, Claude, and Bing Copilot. Verbatim answers captured. Citation graph logged. Brand-mention rate scored. Trend tracked.
  • Reputation Surface (11 sources). Trustpilot, Glassdoor, BBB, Google Business, Yelp, G2, Capterra, ConsumerAffairs, Reddit (per relevant subreddit), industry-trade press, peer-review citations. Daily delta. Sentiment scored. New negative review → same-day alert.
  • Website Analytics. Core Web Vitals, schema.org coverage, EEAT signal completeness, AI-engine bot crawl frequency. Flags pages that AI engines aren’t visiting and pages that Google ranks but AI engines miss.
  • Outbound Email Deliverability. SPF, DKIM, DMARC posture; sender-reputation across major filters; bounce-rate by recipient domain. The conversion-killing problems that go unnoticed until your sales team complains nobody’s replying.

Why these four together, not separately

The four surfaces interact. A negative Glassdoor cluster shows up in Perplexity answers about your hiring brand. A schema.org gap on your Pricing page means AI engines can’t cite your pricing correctly when buyers ask. A failing DMARC record sends your sales emails to spam, which then surfaces as bounce-back data that Reddit threads cite when your brand comes up. None of these surfaces operate independently anymore. Tools that monitor only one of them miss the system, which is where the leverage is.

AiT Pulse exists because we couldn’t buy this combination off the shelf. Brand24 covers reputation but not AI search. SEMrush covers SEO but not AI engines. Postmark covers email deliverability but not website schema. Stitching together five tools and a dashboard is what every agency does and what every agency does badly. We built one thing instead.
